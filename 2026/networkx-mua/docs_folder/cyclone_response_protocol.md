# Cyclone Response Protocol

## Monitoring
The supply chain control tower tracks cyclone systems in the Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea. Cyclone Dana is currently active with high severity, affecting Vizag Port (port_1) and the Chennai-Delhi Land Route (route_2).

## Response Levels
Wind speed below 60 km/h: monitor only, no action. Wind speed 60-90 km/h: issue 48-hour alert, begin reroute planning. Wind speed above 90 km/h: execute immediate reroute of all cargo on affected routes.

## Rerouting During Cyclone
When Cyclone Dana is active, route_2 (Chennai-Delhi land route) is suspended. All cargo originally planned for route_2 is redirected to route_3 (Kolkata-Delhi rail route). Vizag Port operations are reduced to emergency-only berthing. Shipments through Vizag are diverted to Chennai Port (port_2).

## Vendor Notification
All vendors with active shipments on affected routes must be notified within 2 hours of cyclone reaching severe status. Vendors covered under force majeure (see vendor contracts) are exempt from delivery penalties during the cyclone period. Tata Steel (vendor_1) and Reliance Industries (vendor_2) both have force majeure clauses covering natural disasters.

## Post-Cyclone Recovery
Within 24 hours of cyclone downgrade, assess port and route infrastructure. Vizag Port must pass safety inspection before resuming full operations. Route_2 requires road condition assessment before reactivation. Expected recovery time: 3-7 days after cyclone passes.
