# Incident Management SOP

## Incident Classification
Incidents are classified by severity. High severity: complete route or port shutdown, factory offline. Medium severity: partial disruption, reduced capacity, delays under 5 days. Low severity: minor delays, documentation issues.

## Response Timelines
High severity incidents require acknowledgment within 1 hour and an action plan within 4 hours. Medium severity requires acknowledgment within 4 hours and action plan within 24 hours. Low severity is handled in the next business day cycle.

## Port Congestion Procedure
When port throughput drops below 60% of capacity, classify as medium severity. Below 30%, classify as high severity. Immediate actions: assess queue depth, identify alternative ports, notify affected vendors. If Vizag Port is congested, priority berthing is negotiated for critical product shipments (Steel Coil, Steel Sheets).

## Route Disruption Procedure
When a route status changes to "disrupted," all pending shipments on that route are flagged. The operations team identifies the root cause (weather, infrastructure, labor). If the disruption is weather-related, check force majeure clauses in vendor contracts before assessing penalties.

## Factory Maintenance Protocol
When a factory enters "maintenance" status, downstream production is halted. Inventory at connected warehouses is assessed. If stock levels fall below minimum threshold (200 units), trigger emergency procurement from backup vendors.
